# Tribesie PRD

## Executive Summary

Tribesie is a community-driven project design and "pre-crowdfunding" platform.

## Problem Statement

Social media is a lucrative career path, but it is also highly competitive; fame is fleeting.

Consider that a popular influencer often wants to prepare for the future but struggles to do so: any time and energy diverted away from content creation could be detrimental to her earnings during what could be her peak window of fame.

Instead, she continues pushing herself until her popularity growth eventually begins to slow.

She compensates for her lack of preparedness by overworking herself, which causes the quality of her content to suffer, which in turn causes her growth to slow even further.

Her career has entered a 'death spiral' that continues until she is washed up.

---

The solution to such a problem is for the influencer to adopt a strategy that permits her to prepare for the future without sacrificing her current earnings, but rather increasing them, making her life easier, and becoming more satisfying.

With a plan in place, she can then afford to take the necessary breaks between intense work cycles to recharge, and continue her career as an influencer for as long as she wants to remain famous.

Then, when she chooses to step back from content creation, she will be positioned to earn even more money than before.

The model to do so is simple but (as of writing) is rarely adopted as it demands a new way of thinking about business.

---

The legacy "venture" economy is bankrupt—fiscally, technologically, scientifically, morally and spiritually—and can never recover.

This fact is obscured by those with a vested interest in maintaining the illusion of the former system's health as it dies, which includes the entire legacy banking industry.

However, by thinking critically, one may identify that a new system has risen and is consuming the old one.

The nascent "creator" economy is a self-contained entity that exists independently of the economy that preceded it.

It consists of content creators, indie hackers and gig workers, empowered by social media, AI, and blockchain technology, coming together to create and distribute value amongst one another.

The global shift to this new economy is subtle, rare and profound, and it has its own rules and systems which are being discovered.

---

To understand the new system, one may compare the "novice" versus the "master" creator business model.

The "novice" creator business model involves using free social media content to promote a fixed-price subscription directly. This funnel structure echoes the old economy and treats the creator's product as generic, commodified, and impersonal.

The "master" creator business model inserts a crowdfunding campaign between the creator's free content and their paid subscription. Instead of funneling customers from "free" to "fixed"-price value offerings, the customers are funneled from "free" to "flexible" and from there to the "fixed"-price subscription.

In the previous economy, crowdfunding was occasional and individual. In the new economy, crowdfunding should be contiguous and collaborative. An influencer should always be actively promoting a crowdfunding campaign.

This model has a range of advantages and is optimal for creator businesses. It captures the maximum amount of customer value, is participative, and is specialized to the creator and her audience.

To help influencers adopt this new model, we provide a tool that lets them better leverage their own creativity and that of their audience and other creators.

The result is that they make more money, more sustainably, while better serving their audience.

## Target Users

Tribesie caters to three primary user avatars:

1. **The Influencer**: A social media content creator, with a particular focus on popular creators—especially those in the adult-related or high-engagement female creator space.

2. **The Fan**: A supporter of one or more social media influencers, characterized by a younger male demographic and high levels of creative intelligence.

3. **The Organization**: An entity seeking to market itself to the platform’s community, collaborate with members on specific projects, or both.

## Product Solution - Features

###  Proposals

The Tribesie community consists of verified influencers and common users. Verified influencers have access to all features available to common users, along with specialized creator tools. (Either type of user may upgrade to a premium account, as described below.)

Common users publish proposals, often in response to an influencer's request for proposals.

These proposals may be targeted at a specific influencer or set of influencers, or they may be open to anyone.

Influencers can also publish their own proposals, and a user's proposals appear on that user's profile and elsewhere.

Users can upvote proposals (which also acts as a bookmark) or downvote them. The vote score of a proposal starts at 1, as it is automatically upvoted by the proposal creator. Its base score is hidden and is calculated by subtracting the number of downvotes from the number of upvotes. Its active score is displayed to all users and is the highest base score a proposal has historically achieved. Additional downvotes will change the base score but will not affect the active score; however, the active score can change if the accounts or sessions with votes contributing to the base score are deleted or otherwise removed. (This system ensures that creators see their scores only increase or stabilize, preventing "negative fluctuation" monitoring.)

Users can also comment on proposals and reply to other comments. Comments are also subject to the same vote scoring system as proposals.

Proposals are structured into a main section, a rewards section, and a collaborators section. The main section includes a title, description, and media assets. The rewards section must contain at least one "Key Deliverable," and users can add their own proposed deliverables for the project.

The collaborators section can consist of any number of proposed collaborators, including none. Users can then propose collaborators and, for any proposed collaborator, users can propose what role the collaborator will take in the project.

Reward and collaborator proposals are subject to the same voting system as comments and proposals. When a user upvotes or downvotes a specific role for a collaborator, an upvote is transferred to the collaborator if one has not been issued already/will replace a downvote.

Comments are collected under the proposal but can link to specific reward or collaborator proposals, and comments can be searched by what they link to.

Users have a (common) user score which corresponds to the quality of their proposals and comments as measured by the voting system and proposal executions. Users also have trophies which are awarded for specific achievements, such as having a proposal executed.

Premium users can hide their common user score if they wish.

### User Registration

Visitors to the site are able to browse its features (for the most part) as though registered. When they attempt to use a feature, they are prompted to either create a guest session or to pay to register an account.

Guest sessions are temporary. After fourteen days, they are rendered inaccessible and their contents are hidden, including any public interactions, comments or posts. The user then has a further thirty days to roll their session into a paid account or their session is permanently deleted. Users can create as many free sessions as they want (although extreme usage volume is only available with payment) and can roll a free session into a paid account at any time (prior to its deletion).

A guest session is managed using a temporary key which is stored in the user's local storage. It is not tied to the user's personal information and can be deleted at any time.

A paid account is available for a monthly or an annual fee (with a discount for the longer subscription period). A paid account is tied to the user's email address. A paid account is permanent unless the user elects to delete it. Any contents made in a free session is preserved when it is rolled into a paid account and remains so even if the account later discontinues its subscription.

### Token Allocation

A paid user receives a monthly base allotment of tokens and can purchase an unlimited additional amount extra via token packs, which may be allocated to proposals.

### Offers to Execute

Typically when a proposal has reached a certain level of community support, verified influencers can make offers to execute the proposal. They do so by setting a token threshold and selecting from amongst the suggested rewards and collaborators for their offer, including at least the key deliverable. If the influencer tags other collaborators besides themselves, they must also be verified influencers and approve of the offer.

When tokens are allocated to a proposal, the user sets rules as to how and when they may be allocated to an execution offer. The user can decide that any execution offer will be accepted, or only an execution offer that includes specific rewards and collaborators, or that comes from a specific influencer or amongst a set of influencers. This can happen automatically, or the user can receive a notification for manual approval.

Proposals can be terminal or they can be repeating, meaning that unlimited execution offers can be accepted for them. A user can pledge that unallocated tokens will automatically be re-committed to the proposal once an offer has been accepted.

Users are able to experiment with dummy execution offers to see how many tokens would be allocated automatically and with approval based on different configurations of rewards and/or collaborators.

Once an execution offer is made, it has a set number of days until it must be fulfilled (such as seven). If a user's pledged tokens were set to manual approval and the user denies the offer, their tokens are locked until the offer has expired or succeeded. Otherwise, a user can rescind and re-allocate tokens as they please, including to other proposals.

Premium users can enforce token allocation rules, such as only permitting token pledges that will automatically accept an offer, and further customize the length that execution offers can last before expiry.

Once an execution offer meets its threshold, the tokens are transferred either to: (a) the influencer's token wallet, or (b) an escrow, and will be released to form the initial monetary pledge to a secondary crowdfunding campaign on a third-party platform. In the latter case, the tokens will be restored to the user's account if the crowdfunding campaign is unsuccessful. Which of these is indicated depends on the settings selected for the proposal and offer.

### User Verification

In order to prevent influencers from abusing the platform (by creating execution offers and not fulfilling them), a public rating/review system and a multi-tiered levels system are used. Users can set their token pledges to only accept offers from influencers above a certain rating or level.

Users may apply to become influencers, in which case they must verify their identity using ID verification. A verified influencer account can improve its level by executing proposals successfully (and receiving good ratings in response). The higher level an influencer profile has, the higher token threshold it can set for its execution offers, and the more execution offers it can have active at once.

The schedule of ways that a user's level can be improved is publicly available, with each valid action contributing "experience points." Small actions may include promoting the app on social media, large actions may involve a special form of token purchase where the user purchases a large package of special, refundable tokens and pledges them as collateral in case an execution offer fails.

When proposing collaborators for a proposal, they can either be verified influencers or placeholder profiles. A verified user can claim any placeholder profile that belongs to him or her after a process of manual review.

### Premium Users

Users can upgrade to a premium account for an added monthly price. This lets them use a custom domain and branding for their profile and issue their own tokens if desired, as well as other bonus features.

User-issued tokens may only be pledged to proposals on the user's own profile and expire if the user cancels the premium subscription, or can be given an expiry date by the issuer. For instance, a user may issue tokens to each of their premium subscribers on a third-party platform as a reward to be redeemed on our platform.

### Organization Users

Non-influencer users can verify their premium account as an organization, which adds a special badge to the account.

## Product Solution - Design

### Layout

The Tribesie interface is optimized for high-impact visual discovery and intensive collaborative design.

*   **Mobile (Vertical Media Feed)**: Primary discovery uses a vertical scroll similar to TikTok or Instagram Reels. The screen is dominated by the project's "Spark" (main media assets), with floating minimalist controls (Upvote, Comment, Tokens) for quick interaction.
*   **Desktop (The Split Forge)**: On larger screens, the layout transitions to a powerful side-by-side view. 
    *   **The Media Stage (Left Side)**: Displays the project's visuals, immersive plot descriptions, and media assets in a high-fidelity "Comic Book Splash" style.
    *   **The Forge (Right Side)**: A scrollable, interactive panel containing the live data for the project: the Rewards section, the Collaborators area, the Comment stream, and the Token Allocation tools.
*   **Navigation**: A "Compass" menu provides access to the global feed, user profiles, and the user's personal token vault.

### Theme

The visual theme, "High-Fantasy Caribbean Adventure," is designed to make supporting an influencer feel like joining a legendary quest.

*   **Color Palette**: Deep sea teals, tropical aquamarines, sun-bleached parchment backgrounds, and opulent treasure-gold accents for active elements (buttons, tokens, reward highlights).
*   **Typography**: 
    *   **Headers**: Elegant, serif-like "Pirate Age" fonts with subtle comic-book-inspired drop shadows and outlines.
    *   **Body Copy**: Clean, high-readability sans-serif (such as Inter) to ensure that dense functional language about tokens and execution offers remains professional and clear.
*   **Visual Assets**: Profile pages are styled like "Hero Bio" pages from a graphic novel. Interaction feedback (like token allocation or successful funding) is accompanied by subtle, immersive animations—such as a map being stamped or a gold coin toss.
